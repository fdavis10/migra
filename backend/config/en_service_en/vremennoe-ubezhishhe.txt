WHAT TEMPORARY ASYLUM IS

Temporary asylum (TA) is a humanitarian migration status granted to foreign nationals and stateless persons present on the territory of the Russian Federation where return to their country of origin poses a real threat to their life, health, or freedom.

This status is not a residence permit or citizenship, but provides a lawful basis for staying in Russia, exercising basic rights, and being protected against forced return to a country where the risk of persecution exists.

Temporary asylum is granted in accordance with Russian legislation and international humanitarian human rights protection norms.

ADVANTAGES

Obtaining temporary asylum provides the foreign national with important safety and legal protection guarantees:

Lawful stay in the Russian Federation without risk of deportation;
Protection against forced return to a country where a threat to life or freedom exists;
Right to employment on lawful grounds;
Access to medical assistance in state institutions;
Ability to reside in the Russian Federation for the duration of the status;
Social and humanitarian state protection;
Possibility of further legalization (TRP, PRP, citizenship in certain cases).

This status is a form of humanitarian protection recognized in international practice.

WHO MAY APPLY

Temporary asylum is granted to foreign nationals and stateless persons who:

are compelled to leave their country of origin due to a threat to life or safety;
are subject to persecution on political, religious, national, or other grounds;
cannot return to their country due to armed conflicts or humanitarian crises;
face a real threat of torture, violence, or other forms of persecution;
do not qualify for refugee status but are in need of protection.

LEGAL BASES

The legal basis for granting temporary asylum is enshrined in the migration legislation of the Russian Federation and Russia's international obligations.

Main grounds:

Threat to life and safety — if return to the country of origin could lead to physical violence, persecution, or threat of killing or torture.
Political, religious, or national persecution — if the person is under pressure due to political views, religious affiliation, or nationality or ethnic origin.
Armed conflicts and humanitarian crises — in cases of armed conflicts, political instability, or absence of state protection.
Other humanitarian circumstances — if return is contrary to principles of humanity and international human rights protection.

HOW “RESIDENT” HELPS

The process of obtaining temporary asylum requires competent legal and documentary justification of the situation. Errors in submission can lead to rejection even when the actual grounds exist.

Migration service “Resident” provides full support:

situation analysis and assessment of grounds for asylum;
preparation of a legally justified application;
formation of an evidence base (documents, testimonies, certificates);
assistance in drafting explanations and personal history;
accompaniment during document submission to the Ministry of Internal Affairs;
interaction with migration authorities;
accompaniment during case review;
assistance in the event of refusal and its appeal.

Special attention is paid to proper legal argumentation and evidence of threat.

LIMITATIONS

Refusal to grant temporary asylum is possible in cases of:

absence of evidence of threat;
provision of false information;
existence of a criminal record for serious crimes;
threat to state security of the Russian Federation;
attempted abuse of the status;
non-compliance with humanitarian protection criteria.

Each case is considered individually.

PROCEDURE

The temporary asylum registration procedure includes:

Initial situation analysis
Preparation of application and explanations
Collection of evidence of threat
Submission of documents to the territorial Ministry of Internal Affairs body
Interview (if required)
Review of application
Receipt of temporary asylum certificate

The review period depends on the specific region and circumstances of the case.

DOCUMENTS

The basic package includes:

passport or other identity document;
application for temporary asylum;
explanation of reasons for application;
documents confirming the threat (certificates, testimonies, publications, subpoenas, etc.);
migration card and registration;
photographs of the prescribed format;
additional evidence (if available).

The stronger the evidence base, the higher the likelihood of a positive decision.
