WHAT A WORK PATENT IS

A patent is an official document that allows foreign nationals from visa-free countries to legally work in one of the constituent entities of the Russian Federation. The patent is issued for a specified period and grants the right to employment only in the region where it was issued. From 2026, updated rules for patent processing and use came into force, including changes to cost and the patent validity verification procedure.

ADVANTAGES

Legal employment: ability to work in Russia without obtaining a work permit where a patent is the correct route.
Ease of processing: a patent can be obtained without complex bureaucratic procedures if all requirements are met.
Flexibility: ability to renew the patent for a new period without leaving the country.
Transparency: patent validity verification is available online by number or QR code, which is convenient for employers and workers.
Social guarantees: official employment under a patent grants the right to medical care and other social guarantees where applicable.

WHO CAN OBTAIN A PATENT

Foreign nationals from visa-free countries (e.g., Uzbekistan, Tajikistan, Azerbaijan, etc.) who have reached the age of 18.
Persons without a residence permit (PRP) or temporary residence permit (TRP) in the Russian Federation where a patent is the applicable route.
Citizens of EAEU countries (Belarus, Armenia, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan) do not need a patent to work in Russia.

GROUNDS

Confirmation of lawful entry into the Russian Federation (migration card with entry purpose "work" where applicable).
Completion of a medical examination and absence of dangerous diseases.
Passing an exam on knowledge of the Russian language, history, and fundamentals of Russian Federation legislation (with exceptions for privileged categories).
Payment of the state duty and advance personal income tax payments for the patent validity period.
Absence of an entry ban to the Russian Federation and absence of serious administrative or criminal violations.
