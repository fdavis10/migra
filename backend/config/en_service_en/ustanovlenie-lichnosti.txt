WHAT IDENTITY ESTABLISHMENT IS

Identity establishment is an official administrative procedure in which government bodies of the Russian Federation confirm the identity of a foreign national or stateless person in the absence of valid identity documents.

This procedure is fundamental and critically important in migration law, as without an established identity it is impossible to register key statuses: TRP, PRP, citizenship, temporary asylum, and other migration procedures.

Identity establishment is effectively the legal "entry point" into the legalization system in the Russian Federation for persons who lack or possess invalid documents.

ADVANTAGES

Completing the identity establishment procedure opens access to all further migration opportunities:

Possibility of legalization in the Russian Federation even without a passport;
Right to submit documents for TRP and PRP;
Access to applying for temporary asylum or other protection statuses;
Resolution of the foreign national's ambiguous legal status;
Restoration of the ability to legally remain in Russia;
Legal recognition of identity by Russian government bodies;
Further citizenship processing where grounds exist.

Without this procedure, a person effectively cannot function fully within the legal framework of the Russian Federation.

WHO THE PROCEDURE APPLIES TO

The identity establishment procedure applies to the following categories of persons:

foreign nationals who have lost their passport or other documents;
stateless persons (apatrides);
persons whose documents have been destroyed, lost, or declared invalid;
persons unable to obtain documents from their country of origin;
foreigners with expired documents not subject to renewal;
persons who arrived in the Russian Federation without documents for objective reasons.

LEGAL GROUNDS

The ground for conducting the procedure is the impossibility of identifying the person by standard means.

Main legal grounds:

Absence of a valid document — loss of passport; destruction of documents; expiry without possibility of renewal.
Impossibility of contacting the country of origin — absence of diplomatic relations; refusal to issue documents; impossibility of establishing citizenship.
Legal status of a stateless person — absence of confirmed citizenship of any country.
Necessity for migration procedures — a mandatory condition for applying for TRP, PRP, or asylum.

HOW “RESIDENT” HELPS

The identity establishment procedure is one of the most complex in migration law, as it requires a comprehensive approach and the collection of indirect evidence.

Migration service “Resident” provides full support:

situation analysis and determination of a legal strategy;
collection and restoration of any available documents;
preparation of explanations and applications;
formation of a personal identity evidence base;
search for archival data, testimonies, and confirmations;
interaction with Ministry of Internal Affairs bodies and migration subdivisions;
accompaniment during the identification procedure;
legal support in cases of refusals and repeat verifications.

A special feature of the procedure is that identity is established not only through documents but through a combination of evidence.

LIMITATIONS

There are factors that may complicate or delay the procedure:

complete absence of any confirming data;
provision of contradictory information;
impossibility of verifying data through government bodies;
doubts regarding the applicant's identity;
absence of cooperation on the part of the applicant.

Even in such cases, however, identity establishment through comprehensive verification is possible.

PROCEDURE

The identity establishment procedure includes several stages:

Initial legal analysis of the situation
Collection of all available documents and information
Preparation of the application to Ministry of Internal Affairs bodies
Conduct of identity verification
Collection of additional evidence at the request of the bodies
Decision on identity establishment
Issuance of the confirming document

Procedure timelines may vary significantly depending on the complexity of the case.

DOCUMENTS

Depending on the situation, the following may be used:

any preserved documents (passport, copies, certificates);
birth certificates;
documents from the country of origin;
migration card and registration;
photographs;
applications and explanations;
documents confirming personal data (education, employment, family);
witness statements (if required).

Indirect evidence often plays the key role.
