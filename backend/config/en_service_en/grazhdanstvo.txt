WHAT RUSSIAN FEDERATION CITIZENSHIP MEANS

Russian Federation citizenship is the highest legal status, which establishes a full legal connection between a person and the state and provides the maximum scope of rights, freedoms, and guarantees. Obtaining citizenship is the final stage of the migration journey and opens up all the possibilities for a full life in Russia.

After obtaining citizenship, a person becomes a fully rights-bearing participant in all social, economic, and legal processes in the country. The outcome of the procedure is the receipt of a Russian internal passport — the main identity document.

ADVANTAGES OF CITIZENSHIP

Obtaining Russian citizenship provides significantly more opportunities compared to any migration status:

Full legal protection of the state — protection of the citizen's interests within Russia and abroad;
Permanent residence without restrictions — no need to renew status or confirm the right to reside;
Freedom of employment — ability to work in any organization, including government bodies;
Social guarantees — pensions, benefits, payments, privileges;
Access to free healthcare — full range of medical services;
Right to education — including state-funded university places, grants, and scholarships;
Political rights — participation in elections, ability to hold public office;
Freedom of movement — no restrictions on entry or exit;
Possibility of dual citizenship — in certain cases, renouncing previous citizenship is not required.

Citizenship is not merely a status — it is full integration into society and the state.

WHO CAN OBTAIN CITIZENSHIP

Russian Federation citizenship may be obtained by foreign nationals and stateless persons meeting the requirements of the legislation.

Main categories:

persons holding a residence permit (PRP);
persons married to a Russian citizen;
native Russian speakers;
participants in the compatriot resettlement programme;
highly qualified specialists;
citizens of countries with simplified citizenship procedures.

Simplified procedures also exist that allow citizenship to be obtained without extended residence.

GROUNDS

Grounds depend on the applicant's specific situation. The most common grounds are:

residence in the Russian Federation with a residence permit for the prescribed period;
marriage to a Russian citizen;
having close relatives who are Russian citizens;
participation in state programmes;
recognition as a native Russian speaker;
employment in Russia;
study at Russian educational institutions.

The correct choice of ground directly affects the timeline and likelihood of successfully obtaining citizenship.

LIMITATIONS

Obtaining citizenship may be restricted or impossible in the following cases:

outstanding criminal record;
participation in extremist activities;
provision of false information;
violation of migration legislation;
existence of debts, fines, or other legal violations;
absence of a lawful source of income;
threat to state security.

Each case requires individual analysis.

PROCEDURE

The citizenship process includes several stages:

Determination of the ground for obtaining citizenship
Collection and preparation of documents
Submission of application to the Ministry of Internal Affairs
Awaiting decision (timeline depends on the ground — from several months to one year)
Receipt of positive decision
Taking the oath of a Russian citizen
Registration and receipt of the internal passport

Each stage requires strict compliance with legal requirements.

DOCUMENTS

The standard set of documents includes:

valid passport;
residence permit (PRP);
prescribed application form;
photographs;
income documents;
Russian language certificate (if required);
documents confirming the ground (marriage, kinship, programme, etc.);
receipt of state duty payment;
renunciation of previous citizenship (if required).

The list may vary depending on the ground.
