WHAT TRP-O (РВПО) IS

TRP-O is an official document confirming a foreign student's right to temporary residence in Russia. It is issued for the entire period of study plus an additional 180 days for resolving administrative matters or applying for a residence permit. The document is processed at the territorial bodies of the Ministry of Internal Affairs of Russia following the submission of an application and package of documents.

ADVANTAGES

Extended lawful stay: the full period of study plus 6 months after its completion.
Right to work: students with TRP-O may be officially employed in their region of residence where permitted by law.
Exemption from annual stay confirmation: annual check-ins at migration authorities are not required where the law so provides.
Medical care: right to a compulsory health insurance policy and access to medical care.
Applying for a residence permit: upon completion of studies, documents for a residence permit may be submitted when grounds exist.
No need to extend the visa: for citizens of visa-requiring countries, a multiple-entry visa may be issued for the validity period of the TRP-O where applicable.

WHO CAN OBTAIN TRP-O

Foreign nationals enrolled in full-time study at a state-accredited Russian university.
Bachelor's, master's, specialist's, and postgraduate students.
Persons not studying online and not attending preparatory courses only — requirements depend on the programme.

GROUNDS AND REQUIREMENTS

Enrollment in the full-time department of an accredited Russian university.
Submission of a complete package of documents (application, passport, photographs, enrollment certificate, medical certificate, criminal record certificate, etc.).
Fingerprinting and medical examination.
Payment of the state duty.

Important:

TRP-O is cancelled upon transfer to a correspondence form of study or expulsion.
The student must not be outside the Russian Federation for more than 6 months per year unless exceptions apply.
Employment may be permitted only in the region specified in the permit.
