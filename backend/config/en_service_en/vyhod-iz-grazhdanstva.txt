WHAT RENUNCIATION OF CITIZENSHIP IS

Renunciation of Russian Federation citizenship is a voluntary procedure as a result of which a Russian citizen terminates their legal connection with the state and loses Russian citizen status. The service is provided upon the personal application of a legally competent adult, and also in respect of children or legally incapacitated persons — upon application by their legal representatives. The decision is made by authorized Ministry of Internal Affairs or Ministry of Foreign Affairs bodies of Russia, depending on the applicant's place of residence.

ADVANTAGES

Legalization of status in another country: ability to obtain citizenship or other legal status abroad.
Absence of dual liability: termination of obligations to the Russian Federation (e.g., military duty, certain tax and legal norms) where applicable.
Legal certainty: official confirmation of the absence of Russian citizenship for foreign government bodies.
Integration opportunity: simplifies the acquisition of social, employment, and civil rights in another country.

WHO MAY APPLY

Legally competent adult Russian citizens residing both in Russia and abroad.
Parents or legal representatives of minors and legally incapacitated persons.
Persons with no outstanding obligations to the Russian Federation (taxes, fines, court decisions, etc.) and not subject to criminal prosecution — subject to legal requirements.

GROUNDS

Voluntary expression of will by the Russian citizen (personal application).
Absence of outstanding obligations to the Russian Federation (tax, judicial, military, etc.).
Absence of criminal prosecution or an unexecuted conviction.
Possession of citizenship (or guarantee of its acquisition) of another state. Exception — persons for whom renunciation of citizenship is not permitted by law.

Important: The procedure is not permitted where there are outstanding obligations to the Russian Federation or where no other citizenship or guarantee of its acquisition exists.
