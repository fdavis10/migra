WHAT THE INTERNAL PASSPORT IS

The internal passport of the Russian Federation is the main document certifying the identity of a Russian citizen within the country. It is from the moment of receiving the passport that the citizenship registration process is considered fully complete, and the person acquires the ability to fully exercise all their rights.

The passport is required in virtually all areas of life: from employment and access to government services, to opening bank accounts, registering real estate, and entering into legal transactions. Without it, it is impossible to function fully within the country's legal framework.

ADVANTAGES

Obtaining a Russian internal passport gives a citizen full access to all the opportunities the state offers:

Identity confirmation throughout the Russian Federation — the primary document for any legal action;
Official employment without restrictions — ability to work in any company, including government institutions;
Access to banking services — account opening, loans, mortgage;
Access to government services — registration of benefits, registrations, documents;
Right to medical care — access to the full range of free healthcare;
Real estate transactions — purchase, sale, lease;
Registration at place of residence — permanent registration;
Participation in the social system — pensions, payments, privileges.

The passport is effectively the key to all citizens' rights.

WHO CAN OBTAIN THE PASSPORT

The right to obtain an internal passport is held by:

persons who have obtained Russian Federation citizenship;
Russian citizens who have reached the age of 14 (initial issuance);
citizens subject to passport replacement (at ages 20 and 45, or on other grounds).

For foreign nationals, this service becomes relevant immediately upon obtaining citizenship.

GROUNDS

Grounds for passport registration:

obtaining Russian Federation citizenship;
reaching the prescribed age (14, 20, 45);
change of personal data (first name, last name, patronymic);
loss or damage of passport;
identification of errors in the document.

After obtaining citizenship, passport registration is a mandatory procedure.

LIMITATIONS

In most cases, refusal to issue a passport is rare; however, difficulties may arise in cases of:

submission of an incomplete set of documents;
presence of errors or discrepancies in data;
violation of application submission deadlines;
absence of citizenship confirmation;
provision of false information.

Even minor inaccuracies can lead to processing delays.

PROCEDURE

The internal passport receipt procedure includes:

Preparation of required documents
Completion of the prescribed application form
Submission of documents to the authorized body (Ministry of Internal Affairs or MFC)
Awaiting passport production (usually 5 to 10 business days)
Personal receipt of passport

Upon receiving the passport, all data should be checked for accuracy.

DOCUMENTS

The standard set of documents includes:

document confirming Russian Federation citizenship;
prescribed application form;
photographs of the prescribed format;
identity document (if available);
receipt of state duty payment;
documents confirming data changes (if required).

The package composition may differ depending on the situation.
