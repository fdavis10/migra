WHAT DEPORTATION IS

Deportation is a state enforcement measure under which a foreign national is subject to forced removal from the Russian Federation due to the loss of lawful grounds for remaining in the country. Unlike administrative expulsion, deportation is applied under migration legislation and is formalized by authorized bodies, most commonly Ministry of Internal Affairs migration subdivisions.

A deportation decision carries serious consequences: the actual loss of the ability to be in Russia and the automatic imposition of an entry ban for a period of 5 to 10 years. However, in certain cases such a decision may be recognized as unlawful and overturned through judicial or administrative proceedings.

ADVANTAGES OF APPEAL

Timely appeal of deportation allows the rights of the foreign national to be protected and extremely negative consequences to be avoided:

Cancellation of deportation — ability to remain in the Russian Federation on lawful grounds;
Removal or prevention of an entry ban;
Preservation of family, employment, and business in Russia;
Restoration of migration status (TRP, PRP, etc.);
Prevention of forced expulsion;
Protection against unlawful actions by government bodies.

It is especially important to act promptly — appeal deadlines are limited.

LEGAL BASES AND PRACTICE

Deportation may be applied on the basis of Federal Law No. 115-FZ "On the Legal Status of Foreign Nationals in the Russian Federation."

The most common grounds are:

loss or termination of lawful grounds for stay;
cancellation of TRP or PRP;
expiry of visa or permit;
violation of the stay regime;
identification of unlawful presence in the Russian Federation.

Important: not every one of these grounds automatically makes deportation lawful. In practice, violations are frequently encountered:

absence of proper notification of the foreign national;
violation of the decision-making procedure;
disregard of family circumstances (presence of spouses or children who are Russian citizens);
disproportionality of the measure (applying deportation for minor violations);
violation of the right to defense and appeal.

LIMITATIONS

Despite the existence of protection mechanisms, there are cases in which appeal is more difficult:

serious or systematic violations of legislation;
existence of a threat to state security;
forged documents or false information;
missed appeal deadlines;
absence of an evidence base.

Even in such situations, however, mitigation of consequences or a change of legal status is possible.

PROCEDURE

The deportation appeal procedure includes:

Receipt and analysis of the deportation decision
Urgent legal assessment of the situation
Collection of evidence and documents
Preparation of a complaint or statement of claim
Submission to a superior body or court
Participation in court hearings
Receipt of decision to cancel deportation

Important: appeal deadlines are limited (usually 3 to 10 days depending on the situation).
