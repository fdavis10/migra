WHAT A FREE CONSULTATION IS

A free consultation with a migration lawyer at migration service “Resident” is an initial professional review of your situation in the field of migration law of the Russian Federation.

The consultation involves an analysis of documents, the foreign national's current status, and possible legal risks. The lawyer determines which lawful pathways are available in your specific situation: obtaining TRP, PRP, citizenship, challenging government authority decisions, or restoring a lost status.

The primary goal of the consultation is not simply to provide general information, but to formulate a concrete legal action strategy based on current Russian Federation legislation and actual law enforcement practice.

ADVANTAGES

A free consultation provides complete understanding of your migration situation at no cost:

Legal assessment of the situation from the perspective of Russian Federation legislation;
Analysis of risks and restrictions (bans, expulsion, rejection decisions);
Selection of the optimal legalization pathway in Russia;
Understanding of timelines and stages of status processing;
Elimination of errors that could lead to refusal;
Ability to begin document processing immediately after the consultation;
Personal action plan tailored to the specific case.

The consultation allows you to understand your real prospects in advance and avoid incorrect steps.

WHO SHOULD BOOK

The consultation is suitable for all categories of foreign nationals, including:

persons planning to move to Russia;
foreign nationals already present on the territory of the Russian Federation;
persons with expired documents or migration regime violations;
applicants for TRP, PRP, or citizenship;
persons with refusals, entry bans, or expulsion decisions;
employers engaging foreign employees.

WHEN IT IS ESPECIALLY IMPORTANT

Seeking a consultation is particularly important in situations where:

there is no understanding of how to begin legalization in the Russian Federation;
a refusal has been received for TRP, PRP, or citizenship;
there is a risk of expulsion or an entry ban;
documents have been lost or are in a complex status;
lawful stay needs to be restored;
an urgent migration matter requires resolution;
difficulties have arisen with the Ministry of Internal Affairs or migration authorities.

The consultation is effectively necessary in any situation involving legal uncertainty.

WHAT THE LAWYER DOES

Within the consultation, the migration service “Resident” lawyer conducts:

a detailed review of your migration history;
analysis of all available documents;
verification of the lawfulness of the current stay status;
identification of possible grounds for obtaining TRP, PRP, or citizenship;
assessment of risks of refusal, ban, or expulsion;
formulation of a step-by-step action plan;
recommendations on document collection;
explanation of migration legislation in plain language.

Where required, full case management begins following the consultation.

LIMITATIONS

The consultation is of an informational and legal nature and has certain limitations:

does not replace an official decision by government bodies;
does not guarantee automatic receipt of status;
requires provision of accurate information by the client;
complex cases may require additional document analysis;
the final result depends on the legal grounds of the specific case.

PROCEDURE

The consultation process is maximally straightforward:

Contacting migration service “Resident”
Preliminary collection of situational information
Scheduling an in-person or online consultation
Analysis of documents and migration status
Explanation of possible resolution options
Formulation of an individual action plan
Where required — transition to case management

The consultation may be conducted either at the office or remotely.

DOCUMENTS

For the most accurate analysis, it is recommended to provide:

passport or other identity document;
migration card;
registration at place of stay;
current or expired permits (TRP, PRP, visa, etc.);
notifications and decisions from the Ministry of Internal Affairs or court (if available);
refusal decisions (if any);
any additional documents relevant to the case.

The more information provided, the more precise the legal strategy.
