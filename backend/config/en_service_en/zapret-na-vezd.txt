WHAT AN ENTRY BAN IS

An entry ban to the Russian Federation is an official restriction imposed by state bodies that deprives a foreign national of the right to cross the Russian border for a specified period. Depending on the reason, the ban period may range from several months to 5–10 years or more.

It is important to understand that a ban is not always a final decision. In many cases it can be contested and lifted early. This requires competent legal work, knowledge of the legislation, and the correct strategy. Migration service “Resident” provides exactly this kind of assistance.

ADVANTAGES OF TIMELY APPEAL

Timely appeal of an entry ban allows you to:

Restore the right to enter Russia — return to work, family, or business;
Preserve career and income — especially important for foreign employees;
Avoid lengthy waiting for the ban to expire;
Restore migration status — continue processing TRP, PRP, or citizenship;
Protect your rights — if the ban was imposed unlawfully or without justification;
Minimize the consequences of violations — where they exist.

The earlier the appeal process begins, the higher the likelihood of a positive outcome.

WHO NEEDS THIS SERVICE

This service is relevant for foreign nationals who:

have received notification of an entry ban;
learned of the ban when attempting to cross the border;
previously violated migration legislation;
have unpaid fines or debts;
have encountered an erroneous or unjustified decision by state bodies.

The service is also relevant for those planning to enter Russia who wish to check in advance for any restrictions.

COMMON GROUNDS FOR A BAN

An entry ban may be imposed for various reasons. The most common grounds are:

violation of the permitted period of stay in the Russian Federation;
administrative offenses (even several minor ones);
non-payment of fines, taxes, or alimony;
violation of migration legislation;
provision of false information;
decision of security authorities (Ministry of Internal Affairs, FSB, etc.);
previous deportation or expulsion.

It is important to note that not all bans are lawful — many of them can be successfully contested.

LIMITATIONS

Despite the possibility of appeal, there are situations in which lifting the ban may be more difficult:

serious violations of legislation;
repeat offenses;
decisions related to threats to state security;
absence of evidence for appeal;
missed complaint filing deadlines.

Even in complex cases, however, competent legal work can significantly improve the chances.

PROCEDURE

The process of lifting an entry ban includes the following stages:

Verification of the ban's existence and clarification of reasons
Analysis of the lawfulness of the decision
Preparation of documents and evidence
Filing a complaint with the relevant body or court
Review of the application
Receipt of decision on lifting the ban

Timelines depend on the complexity of the case and the chosen method of appeal.

DOCUMENTS

Depending on the situation, the following may be required:

passport of the foreign national;
identity documents;
entry ban decision (if available);
documents confirming lawful stay in the Russian Federation;
evidence of family, employment, or other ties to Russia;
receipts of fine payments (if any);
other documents supporting the applicant's position.

Each case requires an individual approach to forming the document package.
