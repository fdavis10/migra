WHAT REPATRIATION IS

Repatriation to the Russian Federation is a state programme aimed at the return of compatriots and their families to their historical homeland with the possibility of obtaining Russian citizenship through a simplified procedure.

This programme is designed for those who have a historical, family, or cultural connection with Russia and wish to restore their right to live, work, and develop in the country. Repatriation allows the path to citizenship to be significantly shortened and lengthy stages of temporary residence to be avoided.

In essence, this is not merely a migration procedure — it is the legally formalized return home.

ADVANTAGES

The repatriation programme provides some of the fastest and most favorable conditions for obtaining Russian citizenship:

Simplified receipt of Russian Federation citizenship;
Shortened legalization timelines compared to the standard migration order;
Absence of the need for extended residence under a TRP in certain cases;
Right to resettle together with family;
Access to social guarantees and state support programmes;
Ability to immediately integrate into the legal framework of the Russian Federation;
State support during relocation and adaptation (under the resettlement programme).

Repatriation is the most direct path to citizenship for those who have grounds.

WHO MAY PARTICIPATE

Participants in the repatriation programme may include:

former citizens of the USSR and their descendants;
persons with relatives who are Russian citizens;
native Russian language and culture speakers;
compatriots residing abroad;
participants in the State Programme for Voluntary Resettlement of Compatriots;
persons with a historical connection with Russia (through origin or family).

GROUNDS

The grounds for participating in the repatriation programme are based on the fact of belonging to the Russian cultural and historical space.

Historical origin — birth in the USSR or on the territory of the RSFSR; having ancestors who lived in Russia.
Family ties — having close relatives who are Russian citizens; family reunification in Russia.
Linguistic and cultural affiliation — command of the Russian language; belonging to the Russian-speaking culture.
State resettlement programme — participation in the official voluntary compatriot resettlement programme.

HOW “RESIDENT” HELPS

The repatriation procedure requires not only documentary confirmation but also competent legal justification of belonging to the category of compatriots.

Migration service “Resident” provides full support:

analysis of grounds for participation in the programme;
selection of the optimal repatriation pathway;
restoration and confirmation of family ties;
preparation of archival and historical documents;
preparation of the application and document package;
interaction with Russian government bodies and consulates;
accompaniment during case submission and review;
assistance in processing citizenship after programme approval.

We build the entire pathway — from confirming origin to receiving a Russian passport.

LIMITATIONS

Despite the simplified nature of the programme, restrictions exist:

absence of confirmation of origin or family ties;
provision of false information;
existence of a criminal record for serious crimes;
threat to state security of the Russian Federation;
inability to documentarily confirm compatriot status.

Many restrictions can, however, be overcome through competent legal work and the restoration of archival data.

PROCEDURE

The repatriation procedure includes:

Legal analysis of grounds
Document collection and confirmation of origin
Preparation of the programme participation application
Submission of documents to authorized bodies or consulate
Review of application
Receipt of decision on inclusion in the programme
Status processing and subsequent receipt of Russian citizenship

In certain cases the process may be accelerated.

DOCUMENTS

Depending on the situation, the following may be required:

applicant's passport;
documents confirming kinship with Russian citizens;
birth certificates;
archival certificates (USSR, RSFSR, etc.);
documents on ancestors' residence;
confirmation of Russian language knowledge;
migration documents;
photographs of the prescribed format;
programme participation application.

Archival and restored documents often play a key role.
