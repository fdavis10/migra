WHAT A WORK PERMIT IS

A work permit is an official document that grants a foreign national the right to legally engage in employment activities on the territory of the Russian Federation. This document is mandatory for foreign nationals entering Russia on a visa basis and is generally processed with the participation of the employer.

The work permit is a key element in legalizing a foreigner's employment. Without it, work is considered illegal and may entail serious consequences for both the worker and the employer, including fines, visa cancellation, and an entry ban.

ADVANTAGES

Obtaining a work permit provides the foreign national and employer with a number of significant advantages:

Fully legal employment — working in accordance with the legislation of the Russian Federation;
Protection of labor rights — ability to officially defend one's rights in cases of violations;
Stable income — official salary with confirmation;
Grounds for extending stay in the Russian Federation — ability to remain in the country on a lawful basis;
Prospect of further legalization — ability to progress to TRP, PRP, and citizenship;
Reduced risks for the employer — absence of fines and claims from regulatory authorities.

The work permit is thus not merely a formality but a guarantee of safe and stable employment.

WHO NEEDS A WORK PERMIT

A work permit is required by the following categories of foreign nationals:

foreigners entering Russia on a work visa;
highly qualified specialists (HQS);
foreign employees of companies engaged for work in the Russian Federation;
persons without a TRP, PRP, or citizenship of countries with a visa-free regime (for whom a patent is provided).

It is important to note that for citizens of visa-free countries, a patent is more commonly used rather than a work permit.

GROUNDS

The main ground for obtaining a work permit is the existence of an employer willing to officially register the foreign employee.

Key conditions:

conclusion of an employment or civil law contract;
the employer holding a permit to engage foreign workers (in certain cases);
the candidate meeting the position requirements;
compliance with quotas (if applicable).

Without the employer's participation, obtaining a work permit is in most cases impossible.

LIMITATIONS

There are restrictions that may affect the receipt of a work permit:

the employer lacking the right to engage foreign workers;
quota exceedance;
the candidate not meeting the requirements;
presence of migration legislation violations;
provision of false data;
existence of an entry ban for the Russian Federation.

It is also important to note that a work permit is generally tied to a specific employer and region.

PROCEDURE

The work permit registration procedure includes:

Preparation of documents by the employer
Obtaining permission to attract foreign labor (if required)
Processing the entry invitation
The foreign national obtaining a work visa
Submission of documents for the work permit
Receipt of work permit
Execution of the employment contract

Timelines may vary depending on the category of worker and region.

DOCUMENTS

The standard set of documents includes:

passport of the foreign national;
prescribed application form;
employment or civil law contract;
photographs;
medical certificates (if required);
employer's documents;
permission to engage foreign workers (if required);
receipts of state duty payments.

The document composition may differ depending on the situation.
