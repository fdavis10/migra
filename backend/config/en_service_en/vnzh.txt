WHAT A RESIDENCE PERMIT (PRP) IS

A residence permit (PRP) is an official migration status that grants a foreign national the right to permanently reside in the Russian Federation without time limitations. Unlike the TRP, the PRP does not require renewal every few years and effectively equates the foreign national's rights to those of a Russian citizen (with the exception of political rights).

Obtaining a residence permit is a key stage of legalization that provides access to a stable life in Russia, freedom of movement, employment, and social guarantees. It is the penultimate step before obtaining Russian citizenship.

ADVANTAGES OF THE SERVICE

Obtaining a residence permit gives a foreign national the broadest possible opportunities for life and development in Russia:

Permanent residence — the status has no time limit and does not need to be renewed every few years;
Free choice of region of residence — one may live and work in any constituent entity of the Russian Federation without being tied to a specific one;
Official employment without restrictions — no patent or work permit required;
Access to free healthcare — treatment at state institutions on equal terms with citizens;
Right to education — ability to study at schools, colleges, and universities;
Social guarantees — benefits, payments, and the ability to receive a pension subject to conditions being met;
Freedom of entry and exit — ability to cross the Russian border without visas or additional permits;
Ability to apply for Russian citizenship — the PRP is a mandatory step in most cases.

The residence permit is thus a full-fledged status for long-term and stable life in the country.

WHO CAN OBTAIN A PRP

The right to obtain a residence permit is held by foreign nationals who are already integrated into the Russian system and meet the established requirements.

Main categories:

foreigners who have resided under a TRP for a specified period;
highly qualified specialists and their families;
native Russian speakers (NRS);
participants in state resettlement programmes;
persons with close relatives who are Russian citizens;
certain categories of former USSR citizens.

In certain cases, it is also possible to obtain a residence permit through a simplified procedure without prior TRP registration.

GROUNDS

The grounds for obtaining a residence permit depend on the applicant's specific situation. The most common grounds are:

possession of a valid TRP and residence under it for the prescribed period;
recognition as a native Russian speaker;
official employment as a highly qualified specialist;
participation in the compatriot resettlement programme;
having close relatives who are Russian citizens;
education or long-term residence in Russia.

Each ground requires documentary confirmation and proper processing.

LIMITATIONS

There are a number of reasons for which a residence permit may be denied:

violation of migration legislation;
outstanding criminal record;
provision of false or incomplete information;
absence of confirmed income;
identification of dangerous illnesses;
use of forged documents;
violation of permitted stay periods in the Russian Federation.

Even when grounds exist, it is important to fully comply with all requirements.

PROCEDURE

The PRP registration procedure includes several stages:

Determination of the ground for obtaining the PRP
Collection and preparation of documents
Medical examination
Exam (if required)
Submission of application to the Ministry of Internal Affairs
Review of application (usually 2 to 6 months)
Receipt of residence permit

After receiving the PRP, annual confirmation of residence in Russia is required.

DOCUMENTS

The basic set of documents includes:

valid passport;
TRP (if required);
prescribed application form;
photographs;
income documents;
medical certificates;
certificate of knowledge of the Russian language, history, and legislation of the Russian Federation;
documents confirming the ground (marriage, employment, kinship, etc.);
receipt of state duty payment.

The list may be expanded depending on the specific situation.
