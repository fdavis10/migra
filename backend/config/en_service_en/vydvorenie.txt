WHAT ADMINISTRATIVE REMOVAL (EXPULSION) IS

Administrative expulsion is a measure of administrative punishment imposed by a court or border authorities of the Russian Federation, consisting of the forced and supervised transfer of a foreign national beyond the country's borders.

Unlike deportation, expulsion is formalized specifically as a judicial order in an administrative offense case, which makes the procedure for appealing it strictly regulated by procedural law norms.

A decision on expulsion is almost always accompanied by a ban on entry into the Russian Federation for up to 5 years, and in certain cases longer. However, such an order may be overturned with competent legal defense and the existence of appropriate grounds.

LEGAL FRAMEWORK AND PRACTICE

Administrative expulsion is regulated by the norms of the Code of Administrative Offenses of the Russian Federation, in particular Articles 18.8, 18.10, and others.

In practice, expulsion is applied in cases of:

violation of the stay regime;
absence of documents;
unlawful employment activity;
violation of entry or exit rules.

However, judicial practice shows that a significant proportion of orders can be successfully contested on the following grounds:

1. Violation of the principle of proportionality of punishment — expulsion must not be applied automatically; the court is obligated to consider length of residence, family, employment, income, and degree of public danger.

2. Violation of the right to respect for private and family life — under Article 8 of the European Convention on Human Rights, expulsion may be recognized as unlawful if it violates the right to family life.

3. Procedural violations — incomplete examination of evidence; absence of an interpreter; improper notification; consideration without the foreign national's participation; a formal approach without analysis of circumstances.

4. Incorrect qualification of the offense — errors in the application of the norms of the Code of Administrative Offenses may serve as grounds for overturning the order.

LIMITATIONS

Difficulties may arise in cases of:

repeated serious violations of the law;
existence of a threat to state security;
absence of evidence of social ties with the Russian Federation;
missed appeal deadlines (usually 10 days);
voluntary consent to expulsion.

Even in such cases, however, a change of punishment may be possible.

PROCEDURE

The appeal procedure includes:

Receipt of the court order
Urgent case analysis (within the first days)
Preparation of the appeal complaint
Filing the complaint with a superior court
Petition to suspend expulsion
Participation in the court hearing
Receipt of decision (cancellation or modification of the order)

Key point: it is critically important to meet the complaint filing deadline — 10 days from the date the order is issued.

DOCUMENTS

For a successful appeal, the following are required:

court order on expulsion;
passport of the foreign national;
migration documents;
evidence of residence in the Russian Federation;
family documents (especially concerning Russian citizens);
employment contracts;
references and recommendations;
other evidence confirming social integration.
